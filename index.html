ISSF Pistol Sighting Simulator

A browser-based pedagogical tool that models the optics and physics of ISSF open-sight (notch-and-post) pistol marksmanship, shot one-handed and unsupported (free arm). Isolate and observe the sight-alignment (angular) error, arm sway, visual accommodation (front-sight focus), hold, mechanical clicks, wind and cant.

1. Target & aiming zone

A 6-o'clock hold detaches the black front post from the black bull.
The eye focuses on one plane only — always focus on the front sight.

2. Arm sway (wobble) & wind

90° = wind from the right (pushes impact left).

3. Sight alignment (wrist)

Front-post offset within the notch = a wrist angular error. 0.1 mm at the sights ≈ 4.5 mm at 10 m.

Keyboard: A / D
Keyboard: W / S
Keyboard: Z / X

4. Mechanical adjustment (clicks)

Positive = moves impact right/up. 1 click (0.1 mrad) ≈ 1.0 mm at 10 m (2.5 mm at 25 m).
